Project Overview

We envision that future manufacturing systems are distributed close-knit systems in terms of an ensemble of various types of manufacturing processes being operated at different manufacturing stages at different locations, yet they are connected and coordinated in real-time by cyber architecture and analytics. However, most current manufacturing systems, such as semiconductor manufacturing and additive manufacturing, are mainly only studied and optimized at the local level within a single stage. The goal of this project is to establish and validate a cyber-coordinated analytical platform for the efficient scale-up of a future manufacturing system, STREAM (multi-stage distributed future manufacturing system), without loss of quality and/or productivity. Achieving this goal is challenging due to machine heterogeneity and timely communication and computing needs, complex functional manufacturing process dependencies, and highly dynamic and multi-level system interactions. This project will work on three research tasks: 

(1) STREAM architecture design and implementation.

We propose a bridging middleware to enable seamless machine interoperability, and software- and hardware-based accelerator to enable efficient communication and computing.  

(2) STREAM modeling and quality control.

We propose a novel multi-layer functional graphical model to address the high-dimensional functional dependencies over STREAM machines and a spatial-temporal iterative learning control method to achieve an accurate and efficient process quality control.  

(3) STREAM simulation and production control.

To describe the multi-level dynamic interactions, we propose to build a group of coherent and high-fidelity multi-resolution simulators, and with them, hybrid controllers can be designed to control the STREAM production with the consideration of process quality, cost and resource constraints. These research tasks will be validated in an open-source STREAM testbed for high energy density and power density supercapacitor production.
